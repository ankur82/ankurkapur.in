Common Questions
Do I need both courses?
Not necessarily. Personal Finance is the foundation — it covers everything you need to manage your financial life well. Direct Equity is for those who want to go further and invest in individual stocks.
I already know the basics. Is Personal Finance still useful?
Often yes. Most people who know the basics have gaps they are not aware of — insurance sizing, tax structure, nomination. The course is comprehensive enough to fill those.
Is the Direct Equity course suitable for beginners?
It helps to have some familiarity with investing before starting. If you are completely new, start with Personal Finance first — it gives you the foundation the equity course builds on.
Will the content be updated?
Yes. Tax laws change, regulations evolve, and new examples get added. All updates are included in your purchase at no additional cost.
